Current Market Pulse

Hiring Demand

Strong in Enterprise. The focus has shifted from "what was said" to "why it matters"â€”extracting business value from conversations at scale. Contact centers, sales teams, and customer success organizations are investing heavily in speech analytics to improve outcomes, reduce churn, and increase revenue.

Key use cases driving demand:

  • Sales coaching: Analyzing winning vs. losing calls to train reps
  • Compliance monitoring: Ensuring regulatory requirements are met in financial services
  • Customer sentiment: Predicting churn, identifying at-risk customers
  • Competitive intelligence: Tracking competitor mentions and objections
  • Quality assurance: Automatically scoring agent performance

Top Skills

Proficiency in Speaker Diarization, emotion recognition models, and SQL/BigQuery for handling large-scale analytical pipelines. Specific expertise needed:

  • Speaker diarization: "Who spoke when" in multi-speaker conversations
  • Sentiment analysis: Detecting frustration, satisfaction, urgency from speech
  • Emotion recognition: Going beyond sentiment to specific emotions
  • Topic modeling: Clustering conversations by theme, extracting key topics
  • Keyword and phrase spotting: Finding critical moments in conversations
  • Data pipeline engineering: Processing millions of calls efficiently
  • SQL and data warehousing: BigQuery, Snowflake, Databricks for analytics at scale
  • Business intelligence tools: Tableau, Looker, PowerBI for visualizing insights

Compensation

Competitive and stable, often paired with "Product" or "Data Science" titles. Ranges from $150Kâ€“$200K total compensation, with senior roles at enterprise software companies reaching $220K+. The blend of ML expertise and business acumen commands a premium.

Breakdown by role type:

  • Speech ML Engineer: $140K-$180K - Building and optimizing speech analytics models
  • Conversation AI Engineer: $155K-$195K - End-to-end conversation understanding systems
  • Senior Speech Analytics Engineer: $175K-$220K - Architecture, team lead, business impact focus

What Makes This Different from Standard ASR

Speech analytics roles require understanding business context, not just technical accuracy:

  • Business metrics matter: You're measured on revenue impact, not just WER
  • Scale challenges: Processing millions of hours of audio monthly
  • Real-time requirements: Live call analysis for supervisor coaching
  • Integration complexity: Connecting to CRMs, dialers, ticketing systems
  • Explainability: Non-technical users need to understand insights

Industries Hiring

  • Contact Center Platforms: NICE, Genesys, Five9, Talkdesk, Dialpad
  • Sales Intelligence: Gong.io, Chorus.ai (ZoomInfo), Clari, Wingman
  • Customer Success: Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango
  • Financial Services: Banks, insurance companies (compliance monitoring)
  • Healthcare: Telehealth platforms, patient experience monitoring
  • Retail: Customer service quality assurance

Technical Stack You'll Use

  • ASR: Deepgram, AssemblyAI, Google Speech, custom models
  • NLP: Hugging Face Transformers, spaCy, custom sentiment models
  • Data infrastructure: Kafka, Airflow, Spark for pipeline orchestration
  • Storage: S3, Google Cloud Storage for audio files
  • Databases: PostgreSQL for metadata, BigQuery/Snowflake for analytics
