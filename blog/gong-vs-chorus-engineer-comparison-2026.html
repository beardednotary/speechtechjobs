Equity Packages

This is where the real difference emerges:

Gong (Private Equity):

  • IC3: $40K-$70K/year (RSUs at $7.25B valuation)
  • IC4: $70K-$120K/year
  • IC5: $120K-$180K/year
  • Vesting: 4-year, 25% per year (no cliff for most offers)
  • Refresh grants: Annual, typically 20-40% of initial grant
‚ö†Ô∏è Gong Equity Risk

Gong equity is illiquid until IPO/acquisition. Valuation is based on 2024 Series E ($7.25B). If they IPO below this (or never IPO), equity is worth less. However, strong fundamentals ($500M ARR, profitable) suggest good IPO prospects in 2027-2028.

Chorus (Public RSUs via ZoomInfo):

  • IC3: $30K-$50K/year (ZoomInfo stock, NASDAQ: ZI)
  • IC4: $50K-$80K/year
  • IC5: $80K-$120K/year
  • Vesting: 4-year, quarterly vesting
  • Liquidity: Can sell immediately upon vesting (public stock)
‚úì Chorus Equity Advantage

Chorus RSUs are public stock (ZoomInfo, ticker: ZI). You can sell quarterly as they vest. No IPO risk, no liquidity wait. This is worth ~20-30% premium in "certainty value."

Which Has Better Engineering Challenges?

Gong wins on:

  • Cutting-edge ML: Earlier adopter of Whisper, GPT integration
  • Scale: Processing 100M+ calls/year (more data = harder problems)
  • Innovation pace: Ships new ML features monthly
  • Research culture: More papers published at Interspeech, ICASSP

Chorus wins on:

  • Integration challenges: ZoomInfo merger = complex data engineering
  • Proven systems: Less bleeding-edge, more battle-tested
  • Cross-product work: Access to ZoomInfo's GTM data
  • Stability: Less pressure to chase latest trends

Engineering Culture

Gong Culture

Pros:

  • High performers: Surrounded by brilliant engineers, fast learners
  • Ownership: Engineers own features end-to-end (full-stack encouraged)
  • Innovation bias: Encouraged to try new approaches, fail fast
  • Impact: Your work directly affects revenue (high visibility)
  • Growth: Promotion velocity is fast for high performers

Cons:

  • Intensity: "Always-on" culture, hard to disconnect
  • Pressure: High expectations, PIP culture for underperformers
  • Politics: Rapid growth = organizational chaos at times
  • Burnout risk: Many engineers leave after 2-3 years

Chorus Culture

Pros:

  • Sustainable pace: Public company stability, less startup chaos
  • Mature processes: Well-defined eng practices, less firefighting
  • Collaboration: More cross-functional work with ZoomInfo teams
  • Retention: Lower turnover, people stay 3-5 years
  • Support: Better onboarding, mentorship programs

Cons:

  • Slower pace: Less "move fast and break things"
  • Bureaucracy: Public company compliance, more approvals
  • Acquisition integration: Some cultural friction post-ZoomInfo merger
  • Lower upside: Less equity upside, fewer moonshot bets

"Gong is where you go to sprint. Chorus is where you go to marathon. Both are valid choices depending on your life stage."

Career Growth & Promotion

Gong:

  • Promotion cycle: Every 6-12 months for top performers
  • Criteria: Impact-driven (shipped features, revenue impact)
  • Typical path: IC3 ‚Üí IC4 in 18-24 months
  • Management track: Available but IC track equally respected
  • Leveling: Transparent levels, peer-reviewed promotions

Chorus:

  • Promotion cycle: Annual review cycle (more predictable)
  • Criteria: Mix of tenure + impact (slower but steadier)
  • Typical path: IC3 ‚Üí IC4 in 24-36 months
  • Management track: More emphasis on management progression
  • Leveling: ZoomInfo-wide system, more bureaucratic

Winner: Gong for fast climbers, Chorus for steady progression.

How to Decide

Step 1: Know your priorities

Rank these factors 1-5:

  • Total compensation: ____
  • Work-life balance: ____
  • Technical challenges: ____
  • Equity liquidity: ____
  • Career growth velocity: ____

If comp + growth are your top 2: Choose Gong
If balance + liquidity are your top 2: Choose Chorus

Step 2: Interview at both

Get offers from both companies. Use them as leverage to negotiate up. You might find Chorus willing to match Gong's base, or Gong offering better WLB for the right candidate.

Step 3: Talk to current employees

Reach out on LinkedIn. Ask about:

  • Actual working hours (not what recruiter says)
  • Promotion timelines they've seen
  • Team dynamics and manager quality
  • Whether they'd choose the company again

Final Recommendation

For most speech engineers in 2026:

If you're under 35, single/no kids, and ambitious ‚Üí Go with Gong. The comp delta is significant, the learning is unmatched, and you can always dial back later in your career.

If you're 35+, have family, or value stability ‚Üí Go with Chorus. The public stock liquidity, better WLB, and sustainable pace are worth more than the extra $30-50K/year.

If you're on the fence ‚Üí Apply to both, get offers, and negotiate. You might get the best of both worlds.

‚ú® Pro Tip: The Third Option

Consider Gong for 2-3 years (max learning, build savings), then move to Chorus (better WLB, liquidate equity). Several engineers we interviewed did this successfully. You get Gong's comp + Chorus's balance across your career arc.
