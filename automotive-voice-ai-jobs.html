Why Automotive Voice AI?

Voice interfaces are becoming standard in vehicles as drivers demand safer, hands-free control. Modern cars are software-defined vehicles with advanced infotainment systems, and voice AI is the primary interface for navigation, climate, media, and increasingly, autonomous vehicle features.

The automotive voice market is unique because:

  • Extreme noise conditions: Highway speeds (80+ dB), music, passenger conversations, engine noise
  • Safety-critical: Distracting the driver is dangerous; accuracy and low latency are paramount
  • Edge deployment: Processing must happen on-device for privacy and offline functionality
  • Long product cycles: Cars last 10+ years; software must be robust and updatable
  • Global markets: Support for 50+ languages with regional accents

Required Skills & Qualifications

Core Technical Skills

  • ASR fundamentals: Experience with Whisper, Kaldi, or custom architectures
  • Noise robustness: Beamforming, acoustic echo cancellation, speech enhancement
  • Edge AI: Model quantization, pruning, distillation for embedded deployment
  • Programming: C++ (production systems), Python (prototyping, training)
  • Audio processing: Signal processing, microphone arrays, far-field capture

Automotive-Specific Knowledge

  • Automotive SoCs: Qualcomm Snapdragon Auto, NVIDIA DRIVE, NXP i.MX
  • Safety standards: ISO 26262 (functional safety), ASPICE
  • Automotive protocols: CAN bus, AUTOSAR, Vehicle APIs
  • Infotainment systems: Android Automotive, QNX, Linux-based systems
  • OEM processes: Long development cycles (2-4 years), extensive testing

Desirable Experience
